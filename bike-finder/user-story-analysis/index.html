Main Analysis

For our site, a theme we want to include on this website is letting the users feel in control of their actions, whether its by customizing their bike or filtering through our selections. This rings true no matter the level of knowledge each user has of biking. Specifically, our users will most likely have an idea of what they want, whether broad imagery or with a goal in mind.

As such, it's critical we include functionality that emphasizes user control, such as filtered (controlled) navigation and the ability to customize.

However, there's also the issue that many of our users do not know what is the optimal bike for their situation, even if they have a broader purpose in mind. As such, many of the bikes should contain static information that's relevant to a user's concern. For example, a user should be able to tell whether a bike is a mountain bike from first glance. One critical thing to think about when developing the user flow is integrating forms of guidance for the user. Furthermore, the level of guidance should also be controllable. As such, my next steps are looking up references for features that are "controlled guidance".

A good reference is probably going back to the research and scout out our competitors ie. competitive research.
